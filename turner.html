ITI Courses - Turner

Description:

Turner course at ITI provides comprehensive training in precision machining and metalworking techniques. Students learn to operate lathes, milling machines, grinders, and other equipment to manufacture precision components. The curriculum covers topics such as blueprint reading, CNC machining, metallurgy, and quality control.

Duration:

The duration of the Turner course typically ranges from 1 to 2 years, depending on the specific institute and program structure.

Eligibility Criteria:

To enroll in this course, candidates usually need to have completed their 10th standard (or equivalent) education from a recognized board. Some institutes may have additional requirements related to mathematics and science.

Career Opportunities:

Graduates of the Turner course can pursue careers as skilled machinists, CNC operators, tool and die makers, or maintenance technicians in industries such as manufacturing, automotive, aerospace, and engineering. They can work in machine shops, production plants, or specialized manufacturing facilities, contributing to the production of various mechanical components and products.
