Introduction to MBBS

MBBS, or Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery, is a 5.5-year undergraduate degree program that provides a comprehensive understanding of various aspects of medical science, clinical practice, and healthcare. This program prepares students for a variety of career opportunities in the medical field.

By choosing an MBBS degree, you'll gain a thorough understanding of your chosen specialization, develop essential clinical skills, and position yourself for a rewarding career in medicine.

Entrance Exams for MBBS

To secure admission to top medical colleges in India, students may need to clear one or more of the following entrance exams:

  • NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)
  • AIIMS MBBS (All India Institute of Medical Sciences Entrance Exam)
  • JIPMER MBBS (Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education & Research Entrance Exam)
  • CMC Vellore (Christian Medical College Entrance Exam)

Explore the exam patterns, eligibility criteria, and preparation tips to ace these competitive tests.
