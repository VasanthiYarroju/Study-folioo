About MPC Stream

The MPC stream in intermediate education focuses on Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry subjects. It provides students with a strong foundation in science and prepares them for various competitive exams and careers in engineering, medicine, research, and other related fields.

Subjects in MPC Stream

  • Mathematics
  • Physics
  • Chemistry

Scope and Career Opportunities

Students who opt for the MPC stream have a wide range of career options available to them, including:

  • Engineering
  • Medicine
  • Research & Development
  • Aerospace
  • Architecture
  • Information Technology
  • Teaching
  • And many more...

Benefits of Choosing MPC Stream

  • Develops strong analytical and problem-solving skills
  • Prepares students for competitive exams like JEE, NEET, and BITSAT
  • Opens up opportunities for higher education in top universities
  • Provides a solid foundation for pursuing a career in STEM fields

Importance of Maths

In the MPC stream, mathematics holds paramount importance as it serves as the cornerstone for understanding and applying principles in physics and chemistry. Beyond being a subject, mathematics nurtures critical thinking and problem-solving skills crucial for scientific inquiry and real-world applications. Proficiency in mathematics not only prepares students for competitive exams and higher education but also opens doors to diverse career opportunities in fields such as engineering, technology, finance, and research. With its interdisciplinary nature and global relevance, mathematics plays a pivotal role in driving innovation, fostering collaboration, and addressing complex challenges in our increasingly interconnected world.

Statistics on STEM Education and Career Prospects

In recent years, STEM education has become increasingly important due to its role in driving innovation and economic growth. Here are some statistics highlighting the importance of STEM education and the career prospects for MPC stream students:

  • According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, employment in STEM occupations is projected to grow by 8.8% from 2019 to 2029, much faster than the average for all occupations.
  • STEM workers earn higher wages, with the median annual wage for STEM occupations being more than double the median wage for all occupations.
  • In a survey conducted by the National Association of Colleges and Employers (NACE), engineering, computer science, and mathematics were among the top-paying majors for bachelor's degree graduates.
  • STEM graduates have a diverse range of career options, including roles in technology, healthcare, finance, research, and education.
