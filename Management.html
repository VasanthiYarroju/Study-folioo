Introduction to Management Courses After 12th

Management courses after 12th are designed to provide students with a strong foundation in business concepts, leadership skills, and practical knowledge required to excel in the corporate world. These courses offer a blend of theoretical learning and hands-on experience, preparing students for diverse career opportunities in various industries.

Whether you aspire to become a business manager, entrepreneur, marketing specialist, or financial analyst, pursuing a management course after 12th can pave the way for a successful career in the dynamic field of business and commerce.

Popular Management Courses

Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA)

BBA is a 3-year undergraduate program that covers various aspects of business administration, including finance, marketing, human resources, and operations management.

Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com)

B.Com is a 3-year undergraduate program that focuses on commerce, accounting, finance, and economics. It offers specialization options such as B.Com (Hons), B.Com (Finance), and B.Com (Accounting and Taxation).

Bachelor of Management Studies (BMS)

BMS is a 3-year undergraduate program that provides comprehensive training in management principles, business strategy, organizational behavior, and entrepreneurship.

Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM)

BHM is a 4-year undergraduate program that focuses on hospitality management, hotel operations, food and beverage management, and tourism administration.

Bachelor of Retail Management (BRM)

BRM is a 3-year undergraduate program that emphasizes retail operations, merchandising, sales management, customer relationship management, and supply chain management.

Bachelor of Event Management (BEM)

BEM is a 3 year undergraduate program that emphasizes event planning, coordination and management.Specializations: Event planning, Wedding Planning , Corporate Events, Public Relations.

Bachelor of Bussiness Management (BBM)

BBM is 3 year undergraduate program that emphasizes knowledge in bussiness management practices and principles. This course offers some specializtions are Finance, Marketing , Human Resource Management, Entrepreneurship.

Entrance Exams for Management Courses

Admission to management courses in various colleges and universities may require clearing entrance exams. Some of the popular entrance exams include:

  • DU JAT (Delhi University Joint Admission Test)
  • IPMAT (Integrated Program in Management Aptitude Test)
  • SET (Symbiosis Entrance Test)
  • UGAT (Under Graduate Aptitude Test)
  • NPAT (NMIMS Programs After Twelfth)

Each exam has its own pattern, eligibility criteria, and preparation strategies. Make sure to check the specific requirements for the colleges you are interested in.
