ITI Courses - Electronics Mechanic

Description:

The Electronics Mechanic course at ITI focuses on imparting knowledge and skills in the field of electronics equipment maintenance and repair. Students learn about electronic components, circuits, instruments, and systems. The curriculum includes training in troubleshooting, soldering, circuit designing, and equipment calibration. Practical sessions and hands-on training are integral parts of the course to ensure competency in electronic repair and maintenance.

Duration:

The duration of the Electronics Mechanic course typically ranges from 1 to 2 years, depending on the specific institute and program structure.

Eligibility Criteria:

To enroll in this course, candidates usually need to have completed their 10th standard (or equivalent) education from a recognized board. Basic knowledge of electronics and mathematics may be beneficial for understanding the course content.

Career Opportunities:

Graduates of the Electronics Mechanic course can find employment opportunities in various sectors such as electronics manufacturing companies, service centers, telecommunications, IT industries, and government organizations. They can work as electronics technicians, repair technicians, maintenance engineers, or quality control inspectors. With experience and further education, they can advance to roles such as electronics engineer, project manager, or technical specialist.
