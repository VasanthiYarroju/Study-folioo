Diploma in Automotive Technology

A Diploma in Automotive Technology prepares students for careers in the automotive industry, providing them with the knowledge and skills required to work as automotive technicians, mechanics, or service advisors. This program covers various aspects of automotive repair, maintenance, and diagnostics.

Students learn about engine systems, electrical systems, fuel systems, braking systems, steering and suspension systems, and drivetrain components. They gain hands-on experience through laboratory work, workshops, and internships, where they learn how to diagnose and repair common automotive problems.

The curriculum includes courses in automotive technology, automotive electronics, engine performance, and automotive service management. Students may also have the opportunity to specialize in specific areas of automotive repair, such as hybrid and electric vehicles, diesel engines, or automotive diagnostics.

Upon completion of the diploma program, graduates can pursue careers as automotive service technicians, automotive mechanics, automotive service advisors, or automotive parts specialists. They may also choose to further their education by pursuing advanced degrees or certifications in automotive technology or related fields.
